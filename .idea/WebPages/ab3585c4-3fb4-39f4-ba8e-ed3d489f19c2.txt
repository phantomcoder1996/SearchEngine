       <h1>Calling Salma Abu Deif, Help Us Make this Egyptian’s Prom Dream Come True</h1>

      <div class="articl-body-desc"> 
       <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Dear Salma Abu Deif,</span></p> 
       <p>We are writing to you on behalf of a very hopeful and gutsy young soul that decided to go out on a limb and reach out to us to make his one prom wish come true. That is, to take you, Salma Abu Deif, as his prom date. And being the softies that we are, we couldn't help but stick our noses into this particularly novel business.</p> 
       <p>It was a peaceful (read hectic) day at the office as we sat there gradually losing hope in time ever passing and wondering where our food order was, when our thoughts were interrupted by a message on Instagram. 17 year-old Ahmed Rizk - a senior at EBIS - bravely reached out seeking help to fulfill a wish that would make his&nbsp;day/life, as he prepares to head out to the UK once he's done with high school to study computer engineering. So this seemed like a now or never sort of situation.</p> 
       <p>Needless to say we instantly went from dead on the inside to giddy and excited. So on behalf of this spirited young man, we would like to attempt in convincing you to accept his offer. And here's why we think you should be his date for prom (in our humble opinion).</p> 
       <p><strong>Restoring Egypt’s millennials’ faith in love</strong></p> 
       <p>Do it for all those of us who never got to go to their high school prom on account that we got turned down by our crushes or just didn’t get asked out. Honestly, we’re a generation that has grown skeptical of love. And grand love gestures? We’re over them. So you’d be giving love a good name!</p> 
       <p>And we'll start believing that anything is possible again.&nbsp;That tomato factory we wanted to build when we were kids, or that time we wanted to take over the world. All would be possible again...or we’d believe so, at least.</p> 
       <p><strong>Making Ahmed Rizk's dream come true</strong></p> 
       <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">While this is an obvious one, Ahmed Rizk is heading off to London in September to get his bachelors. His impression of high-school as he leaves his native country and boards that plane is entirely in your hands. And since we know a romantic scene at the airport would be asking too much (or would it?) we kindly request you consider the next best thing, which is being his prom date. The airport scenario is a battle for another day.</span></p> 
       <p><strong>Boosting his Rep</strong></p> 
       <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">It would definitely do wonders for Rizk's rep. And it'll give him brownie points with&nbsp;the ladies. That girl who turned him down last Friday? Oh she'll be eating her heart out. Besides we all remember how miserable high-school was in the way of dating, insecurities and so on. You'd be giving his ego a boost and sending him off to university with a close to celebrity high school status.</span></p> 
       <p><strong>Keeping YOUR options open</strong></p> 
       <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">He’s going to the UK to study computer engineering...so you know, he may or may not be the next Steve Jobs.</span></p> 
       <p><strong>First Egyptians to Do it</strong></p> 
       <p>You'd be joining the ranks of Taylor Swift, Rihanna, Jason Derulo and more (although we love you more obvs!) who've all been to prom with a fan. And you'll be the first Egyptian actress/model to do so and he'll be the first Egyptian teenager to actually attempt such a stunt. And we'll obviously be the first publication to put aside all our pressing news reporting to make this prom date happen.</p>
