<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" style="margin:0.5em; background:#cef2e0; font-family:inherit; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3bfb1; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:0.1em 0.6em;">
<div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 157px;"><a href="/wiki/File:HMS_St_Vincent_(1908).jpg" class="image" title="The ship in 1911"><img alt="The ship in 1911" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/HMS_St_Vincent_%281908%29.jpg/157px-HMS_St_Vincent_%281908%29.jpg" width="157" height="100" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/HMS_St_Vincent_%281908%29.jpg/236px-HMS_St_Vincent_%281908%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/HMS_St_Vincent_%281908%29.jpg/314px-HMS_St_Vincent_%281908%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="835" data-file-height="532" /></a></div>
</div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/HMS_St_Vincent_(1908)" title="HMS St Vincent (1908)">HMS <i>St Vincent</i></a></b> was the <a href="/wiki/Lead_ship" title="Lead ship">lead ship</a> of <a href="/wiki/St_Vincent-class_battleship" title="St Vincent-class battleship">her class</a> of three <a href="/wiki/Dreadnought_battleship" class="mw-redirect" title="Dreadnought battleship">dreadnought battleships</a>, built for the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> in the first decade of the 20th century. She was equipped with ten 12-inch (305 mm) guns in five <a href="/wiki/Gun_turret" title="Gun turret">gun turrets</a>. After <a href="/wiki/Ship_commissioning" title="Ship commissioning">commissioning</a> in 1910, her entire career was spent with the <a href="/wiki/Home_Fleet" title="Home Fleet">Home Fleet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grand_Fleet" title="Grand Fleet">Grand Fleet</a>, often serving as a <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Jutland" title="Battle of Jutland">Battle of Jutland</a> in May 1916, the ship discharged 98 shells from her main guns, firing first at the crippled German light cruiser <a href="/wiki/SMS_Wiesbaden" title="SMS Wiesbaden">SMS&#160;<i>Wiesbaden</i></a>. Targeting the battlecruiser <a href="/wiki/SMS_Moltke" title="SMS Moltke">SMS&#160;<i>Moltke</i></a>, her first hit wrecked the <a href="/wiki/Sickbay" class="mw-redirect" title="Sickbay">sickbay</a> and slightly damaged the surrounding superstructure and hull, causing minor flooding. The second hit penetrated the rear armour of the <a href="/wiki/Superfiring" title="Superfiring">superfiring</a> turret at the rear of the ship. Apart from Jutland and the inconclusive <a href="/wiki/Action_of_19_August_1916" title="Action of 19 August 1916">Action of 19 August</a> several months later, her service during World War I generally consisted of routine patrols and training in the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a>. Deemed obsolete after the war, the ship was reduced to <a href="/wiki/Reserve_fleet" title="Reserve fleet">reserve</a> and used as a <a href="/wiki/Training_ship" title="Training ship">training ship</a>. <i>St Vincent</i> was sold for <a href="/wiki/Ship_breaking" title="Ship breaking">scrap</a> in 1921 and broken up the following year. (<a href="/wiki/HMS_St_Vincent_(1908)" title="HMS St Vincent (1908)"><b>Full&#160;article...</b></a>)</p>

<h2 id="mp-otd-h2" style="clear:both; margin:0.5em; background:#cedff2; font-family:inherit; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3b0bf; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;"><span class="mw-headline" id="On_this_day...">On this day...</span></h2>
<div id="mp-otd" style="padding:0.1em 0.6em 0.5em;">
<p><b><a href="/wiki/March_3" title="March 3">March 3</a></b>: <b><a href="/wiki/Hinamatsuri" title="Hinamatsuri">Hinamatsuri</a></b> in Japan</p>
<div style="float:right;margin-left:0.5em;" id="mp-otd-img">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 100px;"><a href="/wiki/File:1875_Carmen_poster.jpg" class="image" title="Poster for the premiere of &quot;Carmen&quot;"><img alt="Poster for the premiere of &quot;Carmen&quot;" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/1875_Carmen_poster.jpg/100px-1875_Carmen_poster.jpg" width="100" height="141" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/1875_Carmen_poster.jpg/150px-1875_Carmen_poster.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/1875_Carmen_poster.jpg/200px-1875_Carmen_poster.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1045" data-file-height="1471" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">Poster for <i>Carmen</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s premiere</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1284" title="1284">1284</a> – The <b><a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Rhuddlan" title="Statute of Rhuddlan">Statute of Rhuddlan</a></b> incorporated the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Wales" title="Principality of Wales">Principality of Wales</a> into England.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1875" title="1875">1875</a> – French composer <a href="/wiki/Georges_Bizet" title="Georges Bizet">Georges Bizet</a>'s opera <i><b><a href="/wiki/Carmen" title="Carmen">Carmen</a></b></i> <i>(poster pictured)</i>, based on the <a href="/wiki/Carmen_(novella)" title="Carmen (novella)">novella of the same title</a> by <a href="/wiki/Prosper_M%C3%A9rim%C3%A9e" title="Prosper Mérimée">Prosper Mérimée</a>, premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Op%C3%A9ra-Comique" title="Opéra-Comique">Opéra-Comique</a> in Paris.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1924" title="1924">1924</a> – The <b><a href="/wiki/Free_State_of_Fiume" title="Free State of Fiume">Free State of Fiume</a></b>, a short-lived independent <a href="/wiki/Free_state_(government)" title="Free state (government)">free state</a> located in the modern city of <a href="/wiki/Rijeka" title="Rijeka">Rijeka</a>, Croatia, was annexed by the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy" title="Kingdom of Italy">Kingdom of Italy</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1943" title="1943">1943</a> – <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a>: During a German aerial attack on London, 173 people were killed in a <a href="/wiki/Stampede" title="Stampede">stampede</a> while trying to enter <b><a href="/wiki/Bethnal_Green_tube_station" title="Bethnal Green tube station">Bethnal Green tube station</a></b>, which was being used as an <a href="/wiki/Air-raid_shelter" class="mw-redirect" title="Air-raid shelter">air-raid shelter</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a> – Motorist <b><a href="/wiki/Rodney_King" title="Rodney King">Rodney King</a></b> was beaten by <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Police_Department" title="Los Angeles Police Department">Los Angeles policemen</a>, causing public outrage that increased tensions between the <a href="/wiki/African_American" class="mw-redirect" title="African American">African American</a> community and the police department over the issues of <a href="/wiki/Police_brutality" title="Police brutality">police brutality</a> and <a href="/wiki/Social_inequalities" class="mw-redirect" title="Social inequalities">social inequalities</a> in the area.</li>
</ul>
